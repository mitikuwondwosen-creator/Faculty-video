Project Context The "Faculty Video Introductions Pilot Program" is designed to address the instructional challenge of enhancing faculty presence in online learning environments at the University of Skaro. With the shift toward remote and blended education, faculty often struggle to establish strong connections with students, which can negatively impact engagement and retention. This module aims to equip faculty with both the conceptual understanding of instructor presence and the practical skills needed to create professional introductory course videos. These videos serve as a vital tool to boost student motivation, clarify course expectations, and foster a welcoming learning environment. Project Purpose This instructional project addresses the challenge of enhancing instructor presence in online learning environments at the University of Skaro. The module supports faculty in creating professional introductory course videos to improve student engagement and learning outcomes. The instruction is designed to be a procedural and conceptual learning experience, helping faculty move from understanding the importance of instructor presence to mastering video editing and publishing skills using DaVinci Resolve, YouTube, and Canvas LMS. This supports the university’s broader goal of improving online teaching quality and student satisfaction. Project Goals • Develop faculty ability to produce engaging introductory course videos following university standards. • Increase faculty understanding of instructor presence and its impact on student engagement. • Equip faculty with practical technical skills in video editing, exporting, uploading, and embedding videos. • Align video content with university branding and pedagogical best practices. Project Audience The primary audience is university faculty members who may have limited experience with video editing and online teaching tools. Learners operate within the university’s Canvas LMS environment and are expected to produce and embed introductory videos as part of their online or blended courses. The instruction supports faculty at beginner to intermediate skill levels.
